Shrimp and Sausage Gumbo

"This Shrimp and Sausage Gumbo is an aromatic and meaty one-pot stew with a rich roux-based sauce. Serve it over rice and enjoy the deep, rustic flavors of Cajun cuisine without having to travel to Louisiana!

Cook Time:1 hour

Serves: 24 people

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Butter, melted
  • 1 1/3 cups All Purpose Flour
  • 4 Yellow Onions, diced
  • 4 Green Bell Peppers, diced
  • 8 Celery Stalks, chopped
  • 16 Garlic Cloves, minced
  • 48 oz. Andouille Chicken Sausage or Pork Sausage
  • 1/4 cup Cajun Seasoning
  • 2 Teaspoons Salt
  • 2 Teaspoons Cayenne Pepper
  • 16 cups Chicken Broth
  • 4 cups Water
  • 7 cups Fire Roasted Diced Tomatoes
  • 8 Bay Leaves
  • 4 lbs. Large Shrimp; raw, peeled, and deveined
  • 6 cups Frozen Okra
  • 1 cup Green Onions (for Garnish)
Instructions

  1. Start by prepping your vegetables:dice the onion, bell pepper and celery and place them in a bowl; set aside. Mince the garlic and set aside separately

  2. Add the Oil and the Flour to a large pot or dutch oven. Turn on the heat to medium and whisk constantly for 20 minutes. This part is crucial that you do not step away, if you want the roux to be correct, this is what you have to do.

  3. Once the 20 minutes is up, add the Onion, Bell Pepper and Celery to the pot and Saute for 5 minutes. Then, add the garlic and saute for 1 minute.

  4. Next, add the Sausage, Cajun Seasoning, Salt, and Cayenne, stir to combine. Then, pour in Chicken Broth, Water, Diced Tomatoes with their juices, and Bay Leaves and bring the Gumbo to a low boil. Simmer uncovered for 50 minutes. Meanwhile, if you haven't already, thaw and peel the shrimp.

  5. Once the time is up, add the Shrimp and Okra to the pot and continue simmering for 10 minutes or until the Shrimp is pink and cooked through.

  6. Remove the Bay Leaves, serve Gumbo in a bowl and garnish with Green Onions. Enjoy!
